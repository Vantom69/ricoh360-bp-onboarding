# RICOH360 Web App: First Steps

Your Business Pack license gives your team access to the RICOH360 web application at [ricoh360.com](https://ricoh360.com). This guide walks you through the essentials for your first week: logging in, browsing your photos, organizing them into albums, using the 360 viewer, applying AI processing, sharing content, and managing your team.

---

## 1. Logging In

When your account is created, you receive an invitation email with a link to set your password. Click that link, create a password, and you are ready to go.

Open [ricoh360.com](https://ricoh360.com) in a web browser (Chrome or Edge recommended) and sign in with your email and password.

A few things to know:

- The web app is your main workspace. Advanced features like Projects, Annotations, and Administration are only available here (not in the mobile app).
- Each Business Pack license includes up to 10 user accounts, so your colleagues can log in with their own credentials.
- If you forget your password, use the "Forgot password" link on the login page.

## 2. The Gallery: Your Photo Library

Once logged in, you land on the **Photos & Videos** section. This is the central pool of all 360 media captured by your team, regardless of which camera or method was used.

**What you see:**
- A grid of thumbnails, sorted with the most recent media first.
- A small icon on each thumbnail tells you whether it is a panorama (image) or a 360 video.

**What you can do:**
- **Upload** media manually from your computer (JPEG, PNG, or MP4, up to 50 MB per file).
- **Open** any thumbnail to launch the 360 viewer.
- **Delete** individual media you no longer need.

**Good to know:**
- The gallery is chronological. There is no folder structure or search bar at this level. To organize your media, use Albums (see next section).
- Tags are generated automatically by AI. You cannot edit or create custom tags.

## 3. Creating and Using Albums

Albums are the primary way to organize your 360 content. Think of an album as a folder you create to group related media: a building floor, a weekly site visit, an inspection report.

![Album list with project thumbnails and metadata](/images/webapp/album_list.png)

**Creating an album:**
1. Go to the **Albums** section in the left menu.
2. Click **Create Album**.
3. Give it a descriptive name (e.g., "Warehouse B - April 2026").

**Adding media to an album:**
- From the gallery, select one or more photos and add them to an existing album.
- Media added to an album still appear in the main gallery. Albums are organizational views, not separate storage.

![Inside an album: photos grouped by capture date](/images/webapp/album_homepage.png)

**Renaming or deleting an album:**
- You can rename an album at any time. Deleting an album removes the grouping, but the media themselves stay in the gallery.

**Albums and cameras:**
If your team uses a THETA X camera with Simple Capture, albums serve a dual role. On the camera screen, the operator selects an album before capturing. Photos then upload directly into that album. This works best with 10 to 15 albums. Beyond that, scrolling through the list on the camera screen becomes impractical.

## 4. The 360 Viewer

Click any panorama or video to open the 360 viewer. This is where the 360 experience comes to life.

![360 Viewer with toolbar: Add to Album, Edit, Share link](/images/webapp/viewer.png)

**Navigation basics:**
- **Click and drag** (or swipe on a touchscreen) to look around inside the panorama.
- **Scroll** (or pinch) to zoom in and out.
- Use the **Home** button to reset the view to the default position.
- Toggle **Fullscreen** for an immersive view.

**Filmstrip:**
At the bottom of the viewer, a strip of thumbnails shows the other media in the same context (the album, project, or gallery you came from). Click any thumbnail to jump to that panorama without going back to the list.

**Auto-rotation:**
Turn on auto-rotation to let the viewer slowly pan through the panorama. This is useful for presentations or when sharing your screen with a colleague.

**Panorama vs. video:**
For videos, you get playback controls (play, pause, scrub) in addition to 360 navigation. However, advanced features like annotations, drawing, side-by-side comparison, and AI processing are available only on panoramas, not on videos.

## 5. AI Processing: Enhance and Blur

The RICOH360 App includes two cloud-based AI tools that you can apply to your panoramas.

![AI Editing panel with Image Enhancement and Blur Person options](/images/webapp/ai_editing.png)

### Super Resolution

Super Resolution enhances image quality by increasing sharpness and detail. This is useful when you need to zoom in closely on a specific area (e.g., reading a label, inspecting a wall surface).

**How to use it:**
1. Open a panorama in the viewer.
2. Click the **Enhancement** button.
3. Select Super Resolution and confirm.
4. Processing takes a few moments. The enhanced version replaces the original in the viewer.

### Face Blurring

Face blurring automatically detects and blurs people's faces in a panorama. This helps with privacy compliance when your captures include bystanders, workers, or visitors.

**How to use it:**
1. Open a panorama in the viewer.
2. Click the **Privacy blur** button.
3. The system detects faces and applies blurring.

**Good to know:**
- Both features work on panoramas only, not on 360 videos.
- Processing is done in the cloud, so results may take a few seconds depending on image size.

## 6. Sharing Content

You can share a 360 panorama or video with someone outside your team by generating a public link.

**How to share:**
1. Open a panorama or video in the viewer.
2. Click the **Share** button.
3. The media is set to "public" and a shareable link is generated.
4. Send the link to your recipient. They can view the 360 content in their browser without needing a RICOH360 account.

![Share link modal with Public/Private toggle and copy link button](/images/webapp/sharing_modal.png)

**Limitations to be aware of:**
- **Individual media only.** You cannot share an entire album or project through a single link. Each panorama or video requires its own sharing link.
- **Annotations are removed.** If you have placed annotations on a panorama, the recipient will not see them. The shared link shows the 360 image only.
- **No password protection.** Shared links are publicly accessible to anyone who has the URL.
- **No expiration date.** Once created, a shared link remains active until you manually revoke it.

**For full team access:**
If someone needs to see albums, projects, annotations, and all other features, the right approach is to invite them as a team member (see next section). Sharing via link is best for quick, one-off previews.

## 7. Team Administration Basics

If you are an admin, you can manage your team from the **Administration** section.

### Inviting members
1. Go to Administration > Team Management.
2. Click **Invite** and enter your colleague's email address.
3. They receive an invitation email and create their own account.

Each Business Pack license includes up to 10 user accounts. You can see how many seats are used and how many are available.

### Roles
There are two roles:
- **Admin**: Full access including team management, camera management, and all platform settings.
- **Member**: Access to the gallery, albums, projects, viewer, and all content features, but no administration rights.

There are no custom roles or sub-teams. Every member sees the same content within the team workspace.

### Monitoring storage
Your license includes 100 GB of cloud storage. You can check usage in the administration console. If you approach the limit, either clean up old media or contact your Ricoh Futures EMEA representative about adding capacity through additional licenses.

## 8. What's Next

Once your team is comfortable with the basics above, the RICOH360 App offers several advanced features worth exploring:

- **Projects and Floor Plans**: Upload a floor plan image and pin your 360 captures to specific locations on the map. This is ideal for site documentation where spatial context matters. See [Projects and Floor Plans](/en/premium-features/projects-and-floor-plans).

- **Annotations**: Place markers directly on a 360 panorama to document observations, flag defects, or add notes for your team. See [Annotations and Markup](/en/daily-use/annotations-and-markup).

- **Side-by-Side Comparison**: Open two panoramas next to each other to compare before and after, or to track progress over time. See [Side-by-Side Comparison](/en/daily-use/side-by-side-comparison).

These features build on the foundations covered in this guide. Start with the gallery and albums, get your team comfortable with the viewer, and move to projects and annotations when your workflow demands more structure.
