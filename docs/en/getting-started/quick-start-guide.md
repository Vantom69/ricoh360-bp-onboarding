# Quick Start Guide

> From unboxing to your first shared 360 photo in about 15 minutes.

This guide walks you through the essentials: setting up your RICOH360 THETA camera, connecting it to the mobile app, taking your first 360 photo, uploading it to the cloud, and sharing it with a colleague. No prior experience needed.

---

## What You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have the following ready:

- **Your RICOH360 THETA camera** (included in your Business Pack)
- **A smartphone** (iPhone or Android) with the RICOH360 app installed
  - iOS: download from the App Store
  - Android: download from Google Play
- **Your RICOH360 account credentials** (email and password sent during onboarding)
- **A WiFi network** the camera can connect to for cloud uploads
- **A USB-C cable** (included) for initial charging

**Practical tip:** Charge your camera fully before your first session. A full charge takes about 2 hours and will give you plenty of battery for your first day of use.

---

## Step 1: Set Up Your Camera

**Estimated time: 5 minutes**

Your Business Pack includes a RICOH360 THETA camera (THETA X or THETA A1, depending on your pack). Here is how to get it ready.

1. **Unbox the camera.** You will find the camera body, a USB-C charging cable, and a soft protective case.

2. **Charge the camera.** Plug the USB-C cable into the camera and connect it to a power source. The LED indicator will show charging status:
   - Blinking: charging in progress
   - Solid: fully charged

3. **Power on the camera.** Press and hold the power button for 2 seconds. The camera will start up and display its status on the screen (THETA X) or through LED indicators (THETA A1).

4. **Check the firmware.** When you later connect the mobile app (Step 2), the app will notify you if a firmware update is available. We recommend updating before your first capture session.

**Practical tip:** Keep the lens cap on when the camera is not in use. The dual fisheye lenses are exposed on both sides and can scratch easily.

For detailed hardware instructions (WiFi configuration, LED meanings, accessories), see the [Hardware Setup](/en/getting-started/hardware-setup) guide.

## Step 2: Connect the Mobile App

**Estimated time: 3 minutes**

The RICOH360 mobile app is your remote control for the camera. It lets you trigger captures, preview shots, and transfer photos to the cloud.

1. **Open the RICOH360 app** on your smartphone.

2. **Log in** with the credentials provided during your onboarding. If this is your first login, you may be asked to set a new password.

3. **Enable Bluetooth and WiFi** on your phone if they are not already on.

4. **Connect to the camera.**
   - In the app, tap the camera connection icon (usually at the top of the screen).
   - The app will search for nearby THETA cameras via Bluetooth.
   - Select your camera from the list. The camera's serial number is printed on the bottom of the device.
   - Once connected, you will see a live preview of what the camera sees.

5. **Configure WiFi on the camera.** The camera needs a WiFi connection to upload photos to the RICOH360 Cloud.
   - In the app, go to camera settings and select WiFi configuration.
   - Enter your office or site WiFi network name (SSID) and password.
   - The camera can remember multiple WiFi networks, which is useful if you work across different sites.

**Practical tip:** If you are on a corporate network that requires a web portal login (captive portal), the camera may not be able to connect. Ask your IT team for a network that uses standard password authentication.

For the full mobile app walkthrough, see the [Mobile App Setup](/en/getting-started/mobile-app-setup) guide.

## Step 3: Take Your First 360 Photo

**Estimated time: 2 minutes**

Now for the fun part. You are about to capture your first complete 360-degree panorama.

1. **Place the camera.** For the best result, hold the camera at arm's length above your head, or mount it on the monopod included in your accessory pack. This keeps your body out of the center of the image.

2. **Check the live preview** on your phone. The app shows a real-time view of what the camera sees. Use it to verify your position and framing.

3. **Set a timer (recommended).** In the app's capture settings, set a 2-second or 5-second timer. This gives you time to step back or hold still after pressing the button.

4. **Capture.** Tap the shutter button in the app (or press the physical button on the camera). Hold steady until the capture completes. You will hear a shutter sound and see a confirmation on screen.

5. **Review.** The captured panorama will appear in the app. You can swipe to navigate around the 360-degree view and check the result.

**Practical tips:**
- Stand at least 1.5 meters away from the nearest wall for the best spatial effect.
- Avoid strong backlighting (do not place the camera directly in front of a window).
- Keep your fingers away from the lenses. The dual fisheye design means both sides capture simultaneously.

## Step 4: Upload to RICOH360 Cloud

**Estimated time: 3 minutes**

Your photo is currently stored on the camera. Now you need to send it to the RICOH360 Cloud so your team can access it from anywhere.

1. **Make sure the camera is connected to WiFi.** Check the WiFi icon on the camera screen or in the mobile app settings.

2. **Start the transfer.** In the mobile app, go to the transfer section and tap "Upload" (or "Transfer to Cloud"). The app will send your photos from the camera to your team's RICOH360 Cloud storage.

3. **Wait for the upload to complete.** Upload time depends on your WiFi speed. A single 360 photo typically takes 10 to 30 seconds on a standard connection.

4. **Verify on the web app.** Open [ricoh360.com](https://ricoh360.com) in your browser and log in with the same credentials. Go to the **Photos & Videos** section. Your panorama should appear at the top of the gallery (most recent first).

**Practical tip:** Your Business Pack includes 100 GB of cloud storage per license. That is enough for several thousand 360 photos. You can monitor your storage usage in the admin section of the web app.

**Good to know:** If your pack is a Business Pack Type S with Simple Capture enabled, the camera can upload photos automatically when connected to WiFi, with no manual transfer needed. See the [Simple Capture Setup](/en/getting-started/simple-capture-setup) guide for details.

## Step 5: View and Share

**Estimated time: 2 minutes**

Your photo is now in the cloud. Let's organize it and share it with a colleague.

### View your photo

1. On [ricoh360.com](https://ricoh360.com), click on your photo in the **Photos & Videos** gallery.
2. The 360 Viewer opens. Click and drag to look around. Scroll to zoom in and out. Use the fullscreen button for an immersive view.

![The 360 Viewer with navigation controls and toolbar](/images/webapp/viewer.png)

### Organize into an album

1. Go to the **Albums** section in the left menu.
2. Click **Create Album** and give it a name that matches your project or site (for example, "Warehouse Floor 1" or "Office Renovation Week 1").
3. Add your photo to the album. You can add more photos to the same album later.

### Share with a colleague

You have two options:

**Option A: Invite them to your team** (recommended for ongoing collaboration).
Go to **Administration > Team Management** and invite them by email. They will get full access to your team's photos, albums, and projects.

**Option B: Share a single photo via link.**
Open the photo in the Viewer, click the share icon, and generate a public link. Send that link by email or messaging. Your colleague can view the 360 photo in their browser without creating an account.

**Good to know:** Shared links give view-only access to a single photo. Annotations you added are not visible to external viewers. For full collaboration with annotations and project access, invite your colleague as a team member (Option A).
