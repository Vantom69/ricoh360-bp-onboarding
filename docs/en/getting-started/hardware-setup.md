## 2. Initial Setup

### Charging

1. Connect the USB-C cable to the camera and plug the other end into a USB power adapter (not included) or a computer USB port.
2. A full charge takes approximately 2-3 hours.
3. On the THETA X, the touchscreen will display the battery level while charging.
4. On the THETA A1, the LED indicator will show charging status (solid light while charging, off when complete).

**Field tip:** Charge fully before your first outing. Battery drains significantly faster when GPS and Bluetooth are both active. If you are doing a long capture session (20+ captures), bring a USB-C power bank.

### Power On / Off

- **THETA X:** Press and hold the power button on the side for 2 seconds. The touchscreen will light up. To power off, press and hold the same button until the screen goes dark.
- **THETA A1:** Press and hold the power button. The LED indicator will turn on. To power off, press and hold again.

### LED Indicators (THETA A1)

The THETA A1 has no screen, so you rely on LED lights:

| LED State | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Solid blue | Camera is on and ready |
| Blinking blue | Processing or transferring data |
| Solid white (charging) | Charging in progress |
| LED off (while plugged in) | Fully charged |
| Blinking red | Error or low battery |

### THETA X Touchscreen

The THETA X has a 2.25-inch touchscreen. After powering on, you will see the live preview. Swipe down from the top for quick settings (WiFi, Bluetooth, battery). Swipe left/right to navigate between capture modes.

### Button Layout

- **THETA X:** Power button (side), shutter button (front face), mode button (side). The touchscreen handles most navigation.
- **THETA A1:** Power button and shutter button only. All configuration is done via the mobile app or web admin console.

## 4. Connecting to Your Phone

### WiFi Connection (Both Models)

The camera creates its own WiFi network. Your phone connects to it.

1. Power on the camera.
2. On the THETA X, swipe down and confirm WiFi is enabled. Note the network name shown (e.g., "THETAX00012345.OSC").
3. On your phone, go to Settings > WiFi and connect to the camera's network. The password is the camera serial number (printed on the bottom of the camera and on the box).
4. Open the RICOH360 mobile app (iOS or Android). The app should detect the camera automatically.

### Common WiFi Issues and Solutions

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Phone does not see the camera network | Camera WiFi is off, or phone is too far away | Ensure WiFi is enabled on the camera. Stay within 5 meters. |
| Phone connects but app does not find camera | Phone auto-switched to a different saved WiFi network | Go to phone WiFi settings and forget or disable auto-join for other saved networks before connecting to the camera. |
| Connection drops during transfer | WiFi interference on busy sites | Move away from other access points. Avoid capturing near routers on the same 2.4 GHz band. |
| "Internet not available" warning on phone | Normal, expected | The camera WiFi does not provide internet. Dismiss the warning. Some phones will disconnect from networks without internet unless you tell them to stay connected. |

**Field tip:** On construction sites, operators often have 3-4 WiFi networks saved on their phone (site office, personal hotspot, etc.). The phone keeps jumping back to a network with internet, breaking the camera connection. Before capture sessions, disable auto-join on all other WiFi networks or temporarily forget them.

### Bluetooth Pairing (THETA X)

1. On the THETA X, go to Settings > Bluetooth and enable it.
2. In the RICOH360 mobile app, go to camera settings and pair via Bluetooth.
3. Once paired, the app can wake the camera WiFi connection automatically when needed.

**Battery impact:** Keeping both GPS and Bluetooth active continuously will drain the battery faster. If you do not need GPS for your captures, consider disabling it to extend battery life.

## 5. Firmware Updates

### Why Update

Firmware updates fix bugs, improve stability, and sometimes add features. Running outdated firmware can cause connectivity issues and missed captures.

### How to Update

1. Connect your camera to the RICOH360 mobile app (WiFi connection).
2. The app will notify you if a firmware update is available.
3. Ensure the camera has at least 50% battery before starting.
4. Follow the on-screen instructions. Do not power off the camera during the update.
5. The camera will restart automatically after the update.

Updates can also be managed from the RICOH360 web app under Administration > THETA Management, where admins can monitor firmware versions across all registered cameras.

**Do not skip firmware updates.** If you experience unexplained connectivity issues, check your firmware version first.

## 6. Camera Settings for Best Results

### If Your Camera Was Pre-configured (Simple Capture / Type S)

Business Pack Type S cameras arrive pre-configured with a capture preset (resolution, HDR mode, timer). You cannot change this preset yourself. The camera shows a simplified screen: select an album, press the shutter button. That is it.

If you need the preset adjusted (e.g., switching from photo to video mode, changing HDR settings), contact your Ricoh account manager. Preset changes require a USB kitting session.

### If You Control Settings (via Mobile App)

**Indoor captures:**
- Enable HDR or HDR Handheld for balanced exposure across bright windows and darker corners.
- Use a 2-second timer to avoid capturing your hand pressing the button. Place the camera on the monopod.
- 11K resolution for maximum detail.

**Outdoor captures:**
- Standard exposure (no HDR) usually works well in daylight.
- Be mindful of direct sunlight hitting one lens, which can cause overexposure on one side.
- Position the camera so the sun is at roughly equal angles to both lenses.

**Low-light situations (basements, unlit spaces):**
- HDR mode is essential.
- Keep the camera as still as possible. Use the monopod, do not handhold.
- Expect some noise in very dark environments with the THETA X or A1. This is a hardware limitation.

**General tips for consistent quality:**
- Hold the camera at a consistent height (roughly 1.5 m / eye level for most documentation).
- Stand at least 1 meter away from the camera when it fires. Use the timer or remote trigger from the app.
- Keep lenses clean before every capture session.
- In tight spaces, anything within 30 cm of a lens will appear distorted.

## 8. Care and Maintenance

### Lens Cleaning

The two fisheye lenses are the most important and most fragile components. Fingerprints, dust, or smudges will appear in every capture.

- Use a microfiber cloth (the type used for eyeglasses).
- Wipe gently in a circular motion.
- Do not use paper towels, tissues, or rough fabric.
- For stubborn marks, use a small amount of lens cleaning solution on the cloth (not directly on the lens).
- Clean before every capture session. This takes 10 seconds and makes a real difference.

### Storage

- Always use the lens cap when the camera is not in use.
- Store in the soft case.
- Avoid leaving the camera in direct sunlight or in a hot car for extended periods.
- If the camera gets wet (THETA A1 has IP64 splash resistance only, not waterproof), dry it with a soft cloth before storing.
