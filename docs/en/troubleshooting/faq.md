# RICOH360 Business Pack - Frequently Asked Questions

This page answers the most common questions from RICOH360 Business Pack customers. If you cannot find what you are looking for here, contact your Ricoh Futures EMEA account manager or visit the [RICOH360 Help Center](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr).

---

## 1. Getting Started

**Q: What is included in a Business Pack license?**

A: Each Business Pack license includes:
- 1 RICOH THETA camera (rental)
- 10 user accounts for your team
- 100 GB of cloud storage
- Full access to the RICOH360 App (capture, viewer, annotations, projects, sharing)
- Ricoh Care hardware coverage (1 camera replacement per contract period)

The camera model depends on your pack type: Business Pack Core includes a THETA A1 or THETA X, while Business Pack Type S includes a THETA X (required for the Simple Capture guided workflow).

> Go further: [Business Package FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/business-package) | [Business Package Type S FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/business-package-type-s)

---

**Q: How many users can access the platform per license?**

A: Each license includes 10 user accounts. These are designed for team-based usage, not individual operators. If your organization needs more users, additional licenses can be added. Each additional license brings 10 more accounts and 100 GB of extra storage.

---

**Q: How do I log in and access my account?**

A: Your account manager will set up your team during onboarding. Once activated, team members receive an email invitation and can log in at [ricoh360.com](https://ricoh360.com). The full platform is accessible via the web app. For field capture, download the RICOH360 mobile app (iOS / Android).

> See also: [Quick Start Guide](/en/getting-started/quick-start-guide)

---

**Q: What happens if I run out of storage?**

A: Each license includes 100 GB of cloud storage. When you approach the limit, you can either manage your existing data (delete old captures you no longer need) or add another license, which provides an additional 100 GB. Your account manager can help you monitor usage and plan for scaling.

## 2. Camera and Hardware

**Q: What is the difference between THETA X and THETA A1?**

A: The THETA X is a standalone camera with a built-in touchscreen that supports the Simple Capture plugin. It captures up to 11K photos and 8K video. The THETA A1 is a newer model available only via rental (Business Pack or Premium Pack). Both cameras transfer content automatically to the RICOH360 cloud when connected to WiFi.

> Go further: [THETA X FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/theta-x) | [THETA A1 FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/theta-a1)

---

**Q: How long does the battery last?**

A: Battery life varies depending on usage (continuous capture, WiFi transfer, ambient temperature). In practice, field users report that heavy capture sessions can drain the battery within a few hours. We recommend keeping a USB-C power bank or charging cable available during long field days.

> See also: [Hardware Setup](/en/getting-started/hardware-setup)

---

**Q: My camera won't connect to WiFi. What should I do?**

A: WiFi connectivity is required for automatic cloud transfer. If your camera does not connect:
1. Check that the camera is connected to a known WiFi network (configured during setup or via the mobile app).
2. Verify that the network allows device connections (some corporate networks block IoT devices).
3. Restart the camera and try again.
4. If the problem persists, use the mobile app to reconfigure the WiFi network.

The camera can store multiple WiFi networks, which is helpful when moving between sites.

> Go further: [WiFi Connection FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faq/ricoh360-app-connection-001) | [RICOH360 App FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/ricoh360-app)

---

**Q: Does the camera have GPS?**

A: GPS accuracy depends on the camera model and conditions. GPS data is embedded when the camera has a signal at the time of capture. Indoor captures typically have no GPS or very imprecise location data. GPS should not be relied upon for precise positioning of captures on floor plans; markers on floor plans must be placed manually.

---

**Q: How do I take care of the lenses?**

A: The THETA's dual lenses are exposed by design (to capture a full 360-degree sphere). Always use the protective case when the camera is not in use. Clean the lenses with a microfiber cloth. Avoid placing the camera lens-down on hard surfaces. A scratched lens directly affects image quality.

---

**Q: What does Ricoh Care cover?**

A: Ricoh Care is included with every Business Pack. It provides 1 free camera replacement during the contract period, with transport included. However, it does not cover loss, theft, or accessories (selfie stick, case, etc.). If your camera needs repair outside of Ricoh Care, contact your account manager.

> Go further: [Repair](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/repair)

## 3. Capture and Upload

**Q: Why do my photos look dark or blurry in low-light conditions?**

A: 360-degree cameras capture light from all directions simultaneously, which makes them more sensitive to low-light environments than standard cameras. For best results:
- Enable HDR mode (configured by your preset or via the mobile app).
- Use additional lighting when possible (a portable LED light can significantly improve results in dark areas like basements or attics).
- Hold the camera steady or use a monopod to reduce motion blur.

---

**Q: How fast does cloud upload take?**

A: Upload speed depends on your WiFi connection quality and the file size. A single 11K panorama is typically 15-25 MB. On a standard office WiFi connection, uploads take a few seconds per image. On slower site networks, it may take longer. The camera queues uploads and sends them automatically once connected, so you do not need to wait on site.

---

**Q: What is Simple Capture?**

A: Simple Capture is a plugin installed on the THETA X that simplifies the capture experience for field operators. Instead of the full camera interface, the screen shows only: album selection and a capture button. This is ideal for non-technical users who need to follow a structured workflow. Simple Capture is included in Business Pack Type S.

Important details:
- Simple Capture works only on THETA X (it requires the touchscreen).
- Each camera has 1 preset (photo or video mode), configured during initial setup by Ricoh.
- The workflow is designed for up to 10-15 albums. If you need more, talk to your account manager about the best organization strategy.
- Initial setup requires a USB connection (kitting by Ricoh), not a remote configuration.

> Go further: [Business Package Type S FAQ](https://support.ricoh360.com/fr/faqs/business-package-type-s)

---

**Q: Can I capture both photos and videos with Simple Capture?**

A: Each Simple Capture preset is configured for either photo or video mode, not both simultaneously. If you need both types of content, the mobile app allows you to switch between photo and video modes freely. Discuss your needs during onboarding so we configure the right preset for your workflow.

---

**Q: What is the maximum file size I can upload?**

A: The maximum file size per upload is 50 MB. Standard 360-degree panoramas are typically well within this limit. High-resolution video files may need to be reviewed; contact your account manager if you encounter upload issues with large files.

## 4. Web App Features

**Q: What are Albums and Projects? What is the difference?**

A: Albums and Projects serve different purposes:
- **Albums** are containers for grouping your captures by any logic that makes sense for your work (site, date, inspection, phase). They are the primary way to organize content.
- **Projects** are spatial hubs built around floor plans. You upload a floor plan image and position your 360-degree captures as pins on the plan, creating a navigable visual map of a space.

Albums do not require floor plans. Projects do. If you do not have floor plans, albums are your best organizational tool.

---

**Q: Do I need a floor plan to use Projects?**

A: Yes. A project without a floor plan has very limited functionality. The project view is built around the spatial layout of a plan. If you do not have a floor plan for a given site, use albums instead to organize your captures. You can always create a project later when a plan becomes available.

---

**Q: Can I search across all my captures?**

A: Currently, there is no global search function in the gallery. You navigate content through albums, projects, or the chronological gallery view. For this reason, consistent album naming is important. We recommend establishing a naming convention during onboarding (for example: "Site Name - Date" or "Building - Zone - Phase").

---

**Q: Can I annotate my 360-degree photos?**

A: Yes. You can place annotation markers directly on any 360-degree panorama. Each annotation has a title and description, and you can also use freehand drawing tools on the image. Annotations are visible to all team members.

Two things to keep in mind:
- Annotations work on photos only, not on 360-degree videos.
- Annotations are visible only within the RICOH360 platform. When you share a panorama externally via link, annotations are not included (see Sharing section below).

---

**Q: Can I compare two captures side by side?**

A: Yes. The side-by-side comparison tool lets you display two 360-degree panoramas next to each other, each with independent navigation. This is useful for tracking progress over time (before/after). Selection is manual: you pick which two panoramas to compare from your album or gallery. There is no automatic date-based matching.

---

**Q: Are AI tags editable?**

A: The platform automatically generates AI tags for your captures (scene type, room detection). These tags are currently read-only. You cannot modify them or add custom tags. If tagging and categorization are important for your workflow, use albums and consistent naming conventions as your primary organization method.

## 5. Sharing and Collaboration

**Q: How do I share a 360-degree capture with someone outside my team?**

A: You can generate a public link for any individual panorama or video. The recipient can view the 360-degree content in their browser without needing a RICOH360 account. Sharing works on a media-by-media basis. You cannot share an entire album or project via a single link.

---

**Q: Why can't the person I shared with see my annotations?**

A: Annotations are stripped from externally shared links. This is a current platform limitation. The recipient sees the 360-degree panorama without any markers, notes, or drawings. If you need someone to see your annotations, the recommended approach is to invite them as a team member (each license includes 10 user seats).

---

**Q: Can I share a full project (floor plan with pins) externally?**

A: Currently, project views (floor plans with positioned captures) cannot be shared via public link. External sharing is limited to individual media. If an external collaborator needs access to the full project view, they should be invited as a team member.

---

**Q: Are shared links password-protected or time-limited?**

A: Shared links are currently public and permanent. There is no option to set a password or an expiration date. Keep this in mind if you are working with sensitive content. For controlled access, inviting collaborators as team members provides a more secure approach.

---

**Q: How do I invite team members?**

A: From the Administration section of the web app, admins can invite new members by email. Invited users receive an activation link and get access to the full platform (based on their assigned role). Each license includes 10 seats.

---

## 6. Administration

**Q: What roles are available for team members?**

A: The platform currently has two roles:
- **Admin**: full access to all features, including team management, camera management, and settings.
- **Member**: access to capture, view, organize, and annotate content.

There are no custom roles, sub-teams, or permission groups at this time. If you need fine-grained access control, discuss your requirements with your account manager.

---

**Q: Can I monitor camera status remotely?**

A: Yes. The Theta Management section in Administration shows all registered cameras with their current status: online/offline, battery level, firmware version, and available storage. You can also manage firmware updates and configure automatic deletion of local files after cloud transfer.

---

**Q: Is there an activity log?**

A: There is currently no activity log or audit trail showing who uploaded, deleted, or shared specific content. If traceability is a requirement for your organization, let your account manager know so we can discuss available options and upcoming improvements.

---

## 7. Measurement and Advanced Features

**Q: Can I take measurements from 360-degree captures?**

A: RICOH360 is a visual documentation platform, not a measurement tool. You cannot extract distances, surfaces, or dimensions directly from 360-degree panoramas. If measurement is a critical need, RICOH360's App Connection marketplace is designed to connect with specialized partner tools that can process 360-degree captures for measurement and 3D modeling.

---

**Q: Does RICOH360 create 3D models?**

A: No. RICOH360 captures 360-degree images and videos. It does not generate 3D point clouds, BIM models, or digital twins in the 3D sense. The platform's strength is accessible, fast visual documentation. For 3D modeling, partner integrations through the App Connection can bridge RICOH360 captures with specialized 3D tools.

---

**Q: What integrations are available through App Connection?**

A: The App Connection marketplace is currently in its early stages. The available integrations are limited at this time. New partnerships are being developed to connect RICOH360 with specialized tools for measurement, 3D, reporting, and other use cases. Check with your account manager for the latest status on available integrations relevant to your workflow.

---

**Q: Can I record video and annotate it?**

A: 360-degree video playback is supported in the viewer (play, pause, navigate). However, annotations, drawing, side-by-side comparison, and privacy blur are only available on panoramic photos, not on video. If documentation with annotations is your primary use case, 360-degree photos are the recommended format.
