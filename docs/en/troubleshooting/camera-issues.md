# Camera Issues - Troubleshooting Guide

> This guide covers common hardware problems with RICOH THETA cameras (THETA X, THETA A1, THETA Z1). Each issue is organized by symptom, likely cause, and solution. If a problem persists after following these steps, contact your Ricoh Futures EMEA account manager.

---

## Camera Won't Turn On

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| No response when pressing the power button | Battery fully drained | Connect the camera to a USB-C charger and wait at least 15 minutes before trying again |
| Camera was working, then stopped turning on | Firmware crash or freeze | Hold the power button for 10-15 seconds to force a restart. If that does not work, disconnect and reconnect the charging cable, then try again |
| THETA X shows a blank screen on startup | Touchscreen boot issue | Wait 30 seconds for the OS to fully load. If the screen stays blank, force restart with a long press on the power button |
| Camera turns on but immediately shuts off | Battery too low to operate | Charge the camera for at least 30 minutes before attempting to use it. On the THETA X, try swapping in a fully charged battery |

**Tip:** The THETA X has a removable battery, so carrying a spare charged battery eliminates most power-related delays on site.

## LED Indicators (THETA A1)

The THETA A1 does not have a screen, so LED indicators communicate the camera's status.

| LED behavior | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Blue solid | Camera is on and ready |
| Blue blinking | Processing or transferring data |
| Red solid | Error or overheating, allow the camera to cool down |
| Red blinking | Low battery, connect charger |
| No LED | Camera is off or battery is completely drained |

For detailed LED codes, check the THETA A1 startup guide (linked below).

## Touchscreen Not Responding (THETA X)

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Touchscreen does not react to taps | Temporary OS freeze | Force restart: hold the power button for 10 seconds, then power back on |
| Touchscreen partially responsive | Dirty or wet screen | Clean the touchscreen with a dry microfiber cloth. Moisture or debris can interfere with touch sensitivity |
| Touchscreen shows a frozen image | Application crash | Force restart the camera. If the issue repeats, update the firmware |
| Screen works but Simple Capture UI is unresponsive | Plugin issue | Restart the camera. If the problem persists, contact your account manager for a kitting reset |

## Battery Draining Fast

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Battery drops to 0% within 1-2 hours | GPS and Bluetooth continuously active | Disable GPS when not needed. Bluetooth can also be turned off when you are not using the mobile app for remote control |
| Battery drains quickly in cold weather | Low temperatures reduce battery capacity | Keep the camera warm between captures (pocket, case). Carry a spare battery for the THETA X |
| Battery drains even when idle | Background processes or outdated firmware | Update to the latest firmware. Adjust the sleep and auto-power-off timers to shorter intervals in the camera settings |
| Noticeably shorter battery life than usual | Aging battery | On the THETA X, replace the battery. On the THETA A1, charge fully and monitor. If the issue continues, contact support |

## Camera Overheating

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Camera displays a heat warning or shuts down | Extended video recording | The THETA X records approximately 25 minutes of continuous video before reaching thermal limits. Pause between sessions to let the camera cool |
| Camera gets hot in direct sunlight | Ambient heat combined with processing load | Move the camera into shade during breaks. Avoid leaving it on a car dashboard or in direct sun when not in use |
| Overheating during upload | Large file transfer generates heat | Allow the camera to finish uploading before starting another capture session. Ensure good ventilation |

**Note:** The THETA A1 is rated for -10C to 40C and has IP64 protection, making it more resilient in extreme conditions. If you regularly work in harsh environments, the A1 may be the better fit.

## Blurry or Dark Photos

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Blurry photos | Dirty or smudged lens | Clean both lenses (front and back) with a microfiber cloth before each capture session |
| Consistently dark indoor photos | Wrong capture settings, no HDR | Enable HDR or HDR Handheld mode for indoor captures. This is configured during kitting for Simple Capture cameras |
| Dark photos in specific rooms | Low ambient light | Use HDR mode and allow the camera to stabilize (use a timer or monopod to avoid motion blur). In very low light, the THETA Z1 with its 1-inch sensors delivers the best results |
| Photos look washed out outdoors | Overexposure | Adjust exposure compensation downward (-0.3 to -1.0 EV). Simple Capture presets are configured by Ricoh during kitting |

## microSD Card Errors (THETA X)

| Symptom | Likely cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Camera does not recognize the SD card | Incompatible or corrupted card | Use a recommended microSD card (high-speed UHS-I or UHS-II). Format the card using the camera's built-in format function, not your computer |
| "Storage full" error despite available space | File system issue | Format the SD card in-camera. Back up existing files to your computer first |
| Photos not saving to SD card | Card is write-protected or damaged | Check the card for physical damage. Try a different microSD card to isolate the issue |

**Note:** The THETA A1 uses internal storage only (~30 GB), so SD card issues do not apply to that model.

## When to Contact Support vs. Ricoh Care Replacement

**Contact your account manager for support when:**
- A firmware update does not resolve the problem
- The camera shows repeated error codes or unexpected behavior
- You need help reconfiguring WiFi or Simple Capture settings

**Ricoh Care replacement applies when:**
- The camera has a hardware defect that cannot be fixed remotely
- The device is physically damaged during normal professional use
- Your account manager has confirmed a replacement is needed

**Ricoh Care coverage:** 1 camera replacement per contract period. Ricoh Care does not cover loss, theft, or accessories (monopod, lens cap, battery, cables).
