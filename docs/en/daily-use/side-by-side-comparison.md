# Side-by-Side Comparison

> Compare two 360 panoramas side by side to track changes, verify repairs, or document progress over time. The comparison view splits your screen into two independent panels, each showing a full 360 panorama that you can navigate separately.

---

## 1. What Side-by-Side Does

Side-by-Side is a comparison mode built into the RICOH360 Viewer. It divides the viewer into two panels placed next to each other. Each panel displays a separate 360 panorama with full navigation (pan, tilt, zoom), and the two panels operate independently. You control each one on its own.

This is not a blended overlay or a slider. It is two complete 360 viewers running in parallel, so you can look at different angles in each panel simultaneously.

---

## 2. How to Activate Side-by-Side

### Step 1: Open a panorama in the Viewer

Navigate to any panorama through your Gallery, an Album, or a Project. Click the panorama to open it in the 360 Viewer.

### Step 2: Enter comparison mode

In the Viewer toolbar, click the **Side-by-Side** button. The screen splits into two panels. The panorama you were viewing stays in the left panel.

### Step 3: Select the second panorama

Use the filmstrip at the bottom of the screen to select which panorama appears in the right panel. Browse through available panoramas and click one to load it on the right side.

### Step 4: Navigate and compare

Each panel has independent controls:
- **Pan and tilt**: Click and drag within either panel to look around.
- **Zoom**: Scroll or pinch within either panel to zoom in or out.
- **Orientation**: The two panels do not synchronize. You can face north in the left panel and south in the right panel.

![Side-by-Side comparison with independent navigation and filmstrips](/images/webapp/side_by_side.png)

### Step 5: Exit comparison mode

Click the Side-by-Side button again to return to the standard single-panorama view.

---

## 3. Selecting the Right Panoramas

The filmstrip displays panoramas from the same context you were browsing (the same album, project, or gallery section). To get the best comparison results:

- **Work within the same album.** If you organize captures by site and date (e.g., "Warehouse Lyon - March" and "Warehouse Lyon - April"), open a panorama from one album, enter Side-by-Side mode, then browse the filmstrip to find the matching shot from the other timeframe.
- **Capture from the same position.** The comparison is most effective when both panoramas show the same physical location. Try to capture from the exact same spot during each visit.
- **Name or tag consistently.** Since selection is manual, clear naming helps you quickly identify matching pairs.

---

## 4. Practical Use Cases

### Construction progress tracking

Open a panorama from your initial site visit on the left. Load a recent capture of the same area on the right. Navigate both to the same angle to see what has changed between visits. This is especially useful for verifying that work has been completed as planned.

### Pre-repair vs. post-repair documentation

Before a repair or maintenance intervention, capture the affected area. After the work is done, capture again from the same position. Use Side-by-Side to create a clear before-and-after record. This pairs well with annotations for flagging the specific areas of interest.

### Seasonal or environmental changes

For outdoor sites, facility exteriors, or infrastructure exposed to weather, periodic captures at the same locations let you monitor changes over time: vegetation growth, wear, weathering, or environmental impact.

### Quality control and compliance

Compare a reference panorama (expected state) with a field capture (current state) to identify discrepancies. This works well for fit-out projects, retail merchandising checks, or equipment placement verification.

## 5. Limitations

| Limitation | What it means in practice |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Manual selection only** | There is no date picker or automatic "show me the same spot two months ago" feature. You browse the filmstrip and select each panorama yourself. |
| **Panoramas only** | Side-by-Side works with 360 photos. It does not support 360 video comparison. |
| **No export** | You cannot export the side-by-side view as an image, PDF, or report. To share a comparison, invite the person as a team member so they can use the feature directly. |
| **No slider or overlay mode** | The comparison is two panels placed next to each other. There is no "wipe" or "slider" mode that blends or overlays two panoramas on top of each other. |
| **Same album recommended** | The filmstrip shows panoramas from your current browsing context. For the smoothest workflow, keep your "before" and "after" captures in albums that are easy to navigate. |

---

## 6. Tips for Better Comparisons

**Establish capture points.** Before your first visit, decide where you will capture panoramas. Mark those positions on your floor plan if you use Projects. On each return visit, capture from the same positions. Consistent positioning makes Side-by-Side dramatically more useful.

**Pair with Projects.** If you use floor plans, your markers show you exactly where each panorama was taken. This removes guesswork when selecting the right "before" panorama to compare with a new "after" capture.

**Standardize album naming by date.** A simple convention like "Site Name - YYYY-MM" helps you quickly locate captures from different periods when browsing the filmstrip.
