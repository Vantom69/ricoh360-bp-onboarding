# Capturing 360 Photos and Videos

> Learn how to take consistent, high-quality 360 captures on site using your THETA camera and the RICOH360 App.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure your THETA camera is set up, connected to WiFi, and registered to your team. If you have not done this yet, see the [Hardware Setup guide](/en/getting-started/hardware-setup).

Your Business Pack license includes a THETA camera (A1 or X depending on your pack) and access to the RICOH360 App for managing your captures.

---

## Indoor vs Outdoor Capture

Indoor and outdoor environments require different approaches. The key differences come down to lighting and space.

**Indoor capture:**

- Use **HDR mode** to balance uneven lighting (windows, dark corridors, artificial light). HDR ensures details are visible in both bright and shadowed areas.
- If using HDR Handheld mode, hold the camera as still as possible during the multi-exposure process.
- Use the **timer** (2s or 5s) so you can step away from the camera before it fires. This avoids capturing yourself in the panorama.
- Position the camera at a **consistent height**, ideally around 1.2 to 1.5 meters. A monopod or tripod makes this easy and repeatable.

**Outdoor capture:**

- On bright days, HDR is less critical but still useful if you are capturing areas with strong shadows (under a canopy, near a building face).
- Watch for wind. A monopod is more stable than holding the camera by hand.
- Avoid direct sunlight hitting the lens from a low angle, as this can create lens flare in the equirectangular image.

For visual demonstrations of indoor and outdoor techniques, watch the MasterClass videos linked in the "Go further" section below.

## Consistent Positioning

Consistency across captures makes your 360 library much more useful over time, especially for before-and-after comparisons or progress tracking.

- **Same height every time.** Mount the camera on a monopod at a fixed extension. Mark the extension point if needed.
- **Same distance from walls.** Stand in the center of each room or at regular intervals along a corridor. Avoid being too close to a wall, which distorts that side of the panorama.
- **Same orientation.** Start each capture with the camera pointing the same direction (for example, always facing the entrance). This makes side-by-side comparison easier later.

## Photo vs Video: When to Use Which

Your THETA camera supports both 360 photo (panorama) and 360 video capture. They serve different purposes.

**Use photo (panorama) when:**

- You need a single, high-resolution snapshot of a space
- You want to annotate, draw, or markup the capture later
- You plan to compare captures side-by-side over time
- You need face blur or AI enhancement processing

**Use video when:**

- You want to document a walkthrough or a continuous process
- You need to show movement, flow, or spatial transitions
- The narrative matters more than a single frozen moment

### Video limitations to know

Video captures in RICOH360 are view-only in the 360 Viewer. You cannot add annotations, draw, apply face blur, use AI enhancement, or compare videos side-by-side. These features are available only for panorama captures.

Recording duration also varies by camera model:

- **THETA X:** Video recording is shorter due to thermal constraints. For longer recordings, use lower resolution settings.
- **THETA A1:** Designed for longer video sessions with better heat management.

If your Business Pack uses **Simple Capture (Type S)**, your camera is configured for either photo or video mode, not both. The mode is set during the initial kitting process.

## Using Simple Capture (Type S)

If you have a Business Pack Type S, your THETA X camera runs the Simple Capture plugin. This simplifies the capture workflow for field operators:

1. Turn on the camera.
2. Select the destination **album** on the camera screen.
3. Press the capture button.
4. The photo transfers automatically to the cloud over WiFi.

Simple Capture works best with up to 10 to 15 albums assigned to the camera. If you need more, consider restructuring your album naming or rotating album assignments. See [Organizing with Albums](/en/daily-use/organizing-with-albums) for best practices.

## Low Light Environments

Some sites have poor lighting: basements, mechanical rooms, construction zones at night. For these situations:

- Always enable **HDR** to pull detail out of shadows.
- Use **exposure compensation** (available in camera settings) to brighten the capture if HDR alone is not enough.
- Avoid moving during the capture. Low light requires longer exposures, so any movement creates blur.
- A stable monopod or tripod is essential.
