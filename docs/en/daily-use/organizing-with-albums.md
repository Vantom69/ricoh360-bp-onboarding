# Organizing with Albums

> Learn how to use albums to structure your 360 captures by site, project, date, or any logic that fits your workflow.

---

## What Is an Album?

An album is a container for grouping 360 panoramas and videos together. It is the primary organizational unit in the RICOH360 App. Albums are flexible: they can represent a location, a project phase, a time period, or any other grouping that makes sense for your team.

Albums serve two roles:

1. **On the camera (Simple Capture):** Albums appear on the THETA X screen as capture destinations. The field operator selects an album before capturing, and photos go directly into that album.
2. **In the web app:** Albums are how you browse, filter, and navigate your 360 content by logical groupings rather than scrolling through a single chronological feed.

---

## Creating an Album

1. Open the RICOH360 App in your browser.
2. Go to the **Albums** section.
3. Click **Create Album**.
4. Give the album a name. Optionally, assign it to a project.

That is it. You can immediately start adding photos to the album or assign it to a camera for Simple Capture.

## Naming Conventions

Good naming makes albums easy to find, especially as your library grows. Here are conventions that work well for different use cases:

| Use case | Naming pattern | Examples |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Site documentation | Location or zone | "Warehouse A", "Loading Dock North" |
| Construction tracking | Phase or week | "Week 12", "Plumbing Inspection" |
| Inspections | Intervention + date | "Inspection 2026-01-15", "Asbestos Survey Building C" |
| Facility management | Building + area | "HQ Floor 3", "Data Center Room 2" |

**Tips:**

- Use dates in YYYY-MM-DD format for consistent chronological sorting.
- Keep names short but specific enough to distinguish albums at a glance.
- Agree on a naming convention with your team before capturing begins.

## Albums as Capture Destinations (Simple Capture)

If your Business Pack is Type S, albums play a direct role in the capture workflow. When you assign albums to a THETA X camera via the admin console, those albums appear on the camera screen.

The field operator:

1. Turns on the camera.
2. Scrolls through the album list on screen.
3. Selects the right album.
4. Captures. The photo lands directly in that album.

![Inside an album: warehouse documentation organized by date](/images/webapp/album_homepage.png)

This flow works smoothly with **10 to 15 albums** per camera. Beyond that, the camera screen becomes difficult to navigate. If you have more locations or interventions, consider:

- Rotating album assignments based on the day's schedule
- Using broader album names that cover multiple sub-areas
- Archiving completed albums and creating new ones

---

## Moving Photos Between Albums

Photos are stored in a global pool. Albums are a layer on top, not exclusive containers.

- You can add a photo from the global gallery to any album.
- Removing a photo from an album does not delete it. It remains in the global pool and in any other albums it belongs to.
- Deleting an album does not delete its photos either.

This means you can reorganize freely without losing any captures.

---

## Album Limitations

Be aware of these current constraints:

- **No nested albums.** Albums are flat. You cannot create sub-albums or folder hierarchies. If you need structure, use naming conventions (e.g., "Site A / Phase 1" as a single album name).
- **No album-level sharing.** You cannot share an entire album via a public link. Sharing works at the individual media level. For ongoing collaboration, invite the person as a team member instead.
- **No advanced sorting or filtering** within an album. Photos display in chronological order.
- **Simple Capture limit.** The camera screen is designed for around 10 to 15 albums. Larger lists become impractical.

---

## Best Practices

### By site
Create one album per physical location. This works well for facility managers or multi-site operators who visit the same places regularly.

### By date
Create albums named by date or period (e.g., "2026-W15" or "April 2026"). This suits teams doing periodic walkthroughs or inspections on a schedule.

### By project phase
Create albums aligned with project milestones (e.g., "Foundation", "Framing", "MEP Rough-in", "Final Walkthrough"). This is natural for construction tracking.

### Hybrid approach
Combine two dimensions in your naming: "Site A / Week 12" or "Building C / Pre-inspection". This gives you both spatial and temporal context in a flat structure.
