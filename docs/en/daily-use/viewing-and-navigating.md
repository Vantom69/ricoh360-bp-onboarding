# Viewing and Navigating 360 Content

> Learn how to explore your 360 panoramas and videos using the RICOH360 Viewer, including navigation controls, filmstrip browsing, and extracting 2D perspectives.

---

## Opening the 360 Viewer

Click on any panorama or video thumbnail in the gallery, an album, or a project. The RICOH360 Viewer opens and displays the content as a full interactive 360 sphere.

The Viewer uses the RICOH360 Viewer SDK for high-performance rendering, so it works smoothly in any modern browser without plugins.

---

## Navigation Controls

### Click and drag
Click anywhere in the panorama and drag to rotate your view. This lets you look in any direction: up, down, left, right, and everything in between.

### Zoom
- **Mouse:** Scroll wheel to zoom in and out.
- **Trackpad:** Pinch gesture to zoom.
- Zooming in lets you inspect fine details. Zooming out gives you a wider field of view.

### Auto-rotation
Enable auto-rotation to have the panorama slowly spin on its own. This is useful for getting a quick overview of the entire space without manual interaction.

### Home Position
Click the Home button to reset the view to the initial position. This is the default angle you see when first opening a panorama.

### Fullscreen
Enter fullscreen mode for an immersive viewing experience. Press Escape or click the fullscreen button again to exit.

## Filmstrip Navigation

At the bottom of the Viewer, you will see a horizontal strip of thumbnails. This is the filmstrip. It shows all the panoramas and videos in the current context (the album, project, or gallery section you were browsing).

Click any thumbnail in the filmstrip to jump directly to that capture. This makes it easy to browse through a series of photos without going back to the gallery.

---

## Setting the Initial Position

The initial position (Home Position) determines which direction the viewer faces when someone first opens a panorama. By default, this is the front of the camera at the time of capture. You can adjust it:

1. Open the panorama in the Viewer.
2. Navigate to the angle you want as the starting view.
3. Set it as the Home Position.

This is useful when sharing captures. You can make sure recipients see the most relevant angle first, such as the main entrance of a building or a specific area of interest.

---

## Panorama vs Video Viewing

The Viewer handles both panoramas and videos, but the experience differs.

**Panorama viewing:**
- Full interactive navigation (click-drag, zoom)
- Access to annotations, drawing tools, face blur, AI enhancement
- Side-by-side comparison with another panorama
- Crop perspective tool (see below)

**Video viewing:**
- Full interactive navigation (click-drag, zoom)
- Play, pause, and scrub through the timeline
- No annotations, drawing, face blur, enhancement, or side-by-side comparison

If you need to annotate or mark up specific moments in a video, capture key frames as separate panoramas and annotate those instead.

---

## Crop Perspective: Extracting a 2D View

Sometimes you need a standard flat image from your 360 capture, for example, to include in a report or send to someone who does not need the full 360 experience.

The **Crop Perspective** tool lets you extract a 2D rectangular view from any angle within the 360 panorama:

1. Open the panorama in the Viewer.
2. Navigate to the angle you want.
3. Use the Crop Perspective tool to select and export a flat image.

This gives you a standard photo that looks like it was taken with a regular camera, but extracted from your 360 capture after the fact. You can choose any direction and field of view.

---

## AI Processing in the Viewer

From the Viewer, you can also apply AI-powered processing to your panoramas:

- **Super Resolution:** Enhance the image resolution and clarity. Useful for older or lower-resolution captures.
- **Face Blur (Privacy Blur):** Automatically detect and blur faces and people in the panorama. Important for privacy compliance, especially in public or occupied spaces.

These processing features apply to panoramas only, not to videos.
