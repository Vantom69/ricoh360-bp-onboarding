# Annotations and Markup

> Learn how to use hotspot annotations and drawing tools to document findings, flag issues, and add context directly on your 360 panoramas.

---

## What Are Annotations?

Annotations are interactive markers that you place directly on the surface of a 360 panorama. Each annotation sits at a specific point in the sphere, so when someone navigates the panorama and looks in that direction, they see the annotation icon.

There are two types of markup available:

1. **Hotspot annotations:** Positioned markers with a title and description text.
2. **Freehand drawing:** A drawing tool that lets you sketch directly on the panorama surface.

Both tools are accessed from within the 360 Viewer.

---

## Creating a Hotspot Annotation

1. Open a panorama in the 360 Viewer.
2. Navigate to the area you want to annotate.
3. Click the **Annotation** tool in the toolbar.
4. Click on the specific point in the panorama where you want to place the marker.
5. Enter a **title** (short label, e.g., "Crack in wall" or "Fire extinguisher location").
6. Enter a **description** (optional, for longer notes or context).
7. Save.

The annotation appears as a hotspot icon in the panorama. Anyone on your team who opens this panorama will see it.

---

## Editing and Deleting Annotations

- **Edit:** Click on an existing annotation to open its details. Modify the title or description and save.
- **Delete:** Open the annotation details and select delete. This removes it permanently.

Annotations are tied to the panorama. If you delete the panorama, its annotations go with it.

---

## Using the Drawing Tool

The drawing tool lets you sketch freehand lines and shapes directly on the panorama surface. This is useful for circling an area of interest, drawing arrows to highlight something, or marking boundaries.

1. Open a panorama in the 360 Viewer.
2. Select the **Drawing** tool from the toolbar.
3. Draw directly on the panorama with your mouse or trackpad.

Drawings are saved as part of the panorama's markup layer, alongside hotspot annotations.

---

## Use Cases

Annotations and drawing are most valuable when you need to turn a 360 capture into a documented record, not just a picture.

### Flagging defects
Place a hotspot on a crack, a water stain, a damaged component, or any issue that needs attention. Add a description with details: severity, recommended action, reference number.

### Marking points of interest
Identify specific equipment, access points, safety installations, or measurement locations. This turns your panorama into a reference document that anyone on the team can consult.

### Documenting findings during inspections
Walk through a site, capture panoramas, and annotate key observations on the spot (or later from the office). Each annotation becomes a geolocated note within the visual context of the space.

### Construction progress notes
Mark completed work, pending items, or areas that need rework. Combined with the side-by-side comparison feature, this gives you a visual timeline with documented context at each point.

---

## Limitations

Be aware of these current constraints when using annotations:

| Limitation | What it means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Panoramas only** | Annotations and drawing work on 360 panorama images only. They are not available on 360 videos. If you need to annotate a moment from a video, capture it as a separate panorama. |
| **Not visible in share links** | When you share a panorama via a public link, annotations are stripped. External viewers see the raw 360 image without any markup. |
| **Team-internal only** | Annotations are visible to members of your team. They are not shared across separate teams or organizations. If you need an external party to see annotations, they must be invited as a team member. |
| **No categories or types** | All annotations look the same. There is no way to tag an annotation as "defect", "info", or "alert" with different icons or colors. Use the title field to indicate the type (e.g., prefix with "DEFECT:" or "NOTE:"). |
| **No threaded discussion** | Annotations are one-way markers. There is no reply or comment thread on an annotation. For discussion, use your usual communication tools and reference the annotation by its title and location. |

---

## Tips for Effective Annotations

- **Be specific in titles.** "Crack" is less useful than "Horizontal crack above door frame, approx. 30 cm". Future you (and your colleagues) will thank you.
- **Use consistent naming.** If your team agrees on prefixes (DEFECT, NOTE, MEASURE, SAFETY), annotations become scannable and easier to filter mentally.
- **Annotate the same day as capture.** Details fade from memory quickly. Mark up your panoramas while the site visit is still fresh.
- **Combine with drawings.** Use a hotspot for the label and a drawing to circle the relevant area. Together, they make the annotation unmistakable.
