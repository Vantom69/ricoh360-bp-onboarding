# Inspection and Compliance Reporting

> End-to-end guide for using RICOH360 Business Pack to document conditions, build evidence packages, and support audits, regulatory compliance, or insurance claims.

---

## 1. Who This Guide Is For

This guide is designed for professionals whose work depends on thorough, defensible visual documentation:

- **Infrastructure inspectors** conducting periodic assessments of facilities, equipment, or buildings
- **Compliance officers** overseeing regulatory audits and ensuring documentation meets standards
- **Quality managers** tracking conditions across sites, flagging non-conformances, and verifying corrective actions
- **Insurance adjusters and loss experts** documenting claims, assessing damage, and building evidence files
- **Diagnosticians** (asbestos, lead, energy performance) performing high-frequency interventions with strict reporting requirements

Whether you perform 5 inspections a day or 50 deep assessments a year, the core challenge is the same: capturing complete, unambiguous visual evidence and turning it into a reliable record.

---

## 2. The Challenge

Most inspection teams still rely on 2D smartphone photos, manually organized in folders or pasted into reports. This creates recurring problems:

- **Incomplete records.** Standard photos capture fragments. You photograph a crack in a wall but lose the surrounding context. A month later, when a finding is disputed, there is no way to verify what the rest of the room looked like.
- **Multiple revisits.** "Did I capture the ceiling in room 3?" Uncertainty about coverage forces return visits, costing time and travel budget.
- **Privacy requirements.** Inspections in occupied buildings, public spaces, or healthcare facilities must comply with GDPR and privacy regulations. Faces and identifiable individuals in photos create liability.
- **Disputed findings.** Without comprehensive visual evidence, clients, insurers, or regulatory bodies can challenge inspection conclusions. The burden of proof falls on the inspector.
- **Context lost between field and desk.** The gap between capturing on-site and writing the report at the office erases spatial memory. Flat photos do not reconstruct the environment.

360 panoramic capture addresses these problems at the source: one shot captures the entire space, preserving full context for anyone reviewing the evidence later.

## 3. Recommended Setup

### Choosing the right camera

Inspection work has specific requirements around environment, speed, and operator skill level.

**Will non-technical operators use Simple Capture (Type S)?**
If inspectors need to select an album on the camera screen, press the button, and have everything auto-upload without a phone, **THETA X is required**. Simple Capture is not available on the THETA A1. This matters for teams with many field operators who need a simple, guided capture workflow.

**Is video capture a primary need?**
If your inspections involve long video walkthroughs (documenting building systems, recording a continuous path through a facility), the **THETA A1** offers up to 120 minutes of continuous video recording versus roughly 25 minutes on the THETA X. For panoramic photo capture, which is the standard mode for most inspection work, both cameras perform equally well.

**Are inspections in harsh environments?**
Dusty construction sites, damp basements, industrial facilities, outdoor infrastructure in rain or cold. The **THETA A1** is IP64-rated (dust-tight, splash-resistant) and operates from -10C to 40C. The THETA X has no IP rating. If your inspections regularly take you into harsh conditions, the A1 is the safer choice.

For a full feature comparison, see: [Camera Comparison](/en/reference/camera-comparison)

### Choosing your accessories

Inspection work typically favors consistency and speed.

- **TM-2 (monopod/selfie stick):** The default choice for most inspectors. Lightweight, fast to deploy, easy to carry between rooms and floors. Keeps the camera at a consistent height during systematic room-by-room capture.
- **TD-2 (tripod/monopod hybrid):** Use when you need the camera to stand by itself at fixed positions, such as recurring inspections where you always capture from the exact same spots. Heavier but provides better stability.
- **Both TM-2 and TD-2:** Useful for inspection teams that combine quick walkthroughs (TM-2) with fixed-position documentation at key locations (TD-2).
- **Helmet mount (TM-2 adapter):** Hands-free capture for hard-hat environments. Useful when inspecting while climbing, holding plans, or using measurement tools simultaneously.

### Album structure

Create **one album per inspection** (or per site visit for recurring assessments). This keeps each intervention self-contained and easy to locate later.

**Naming convention:** `YYYY-MM-DD_SiteName_InspectionType`

Examples:
- `2026-04-10_Warehouse-Rungis_Annual-Safety`
- `2026-04-12_Building-B_Asbestos-Diagnostic`
- `2026-04-14_Bridge-A47_Structural-Assessment`

Consistent naming makes it straightforward to find past inspections, compare year-over-year records, and hand off documentation to colleagues or clients.

### Team setup

Ensure all inspectors on your team have their RICOH360 accounts configured before field deployment. See the [Quick Start Guide](/en/getting-started/quick-start-guide) for initial setup.

## 4. Inspection Workflow

### Before the inspection

1. **Create an album** in the RICOH360 web app with the naming convention above.
2. **Charge the camera.** A full charge provides enough battery for a full day of panoramic captures.
3. **Review previous inspections** if this is a recurring site. Open last year's album to refresh your memory on known issues and compare conditions after your new capture.

**Practical tip:** If you use a THETA X with Simple Capture, assign the new album to your camera before leaving the office. The album will appear on the camera screen, ready for selection on arrival.

### During the inspection

1. **Capture systematically.** Move through the space room by room or zone by zone. One panorama per room or area of interest gives complete coverage. For large spaces, capture from multiple positions.
2. **Position the camera consistently.** Use a monopod at a fixed height (approximately 150 cm) for repeatable framing. This is especially important for recurring inspections where you will compare images over time.
3. **Prioritize coverage over perfection.** A 360 panorama captures everything in the sphere. You do not need to frame individual defects during capture. You will zoom in and annotate them later from the web app.

**Practical tip:** For basements, crawl spaces, and poorly lit areas, enable **HDR mode** on the camera. This dramatically improves image quality in mixed or low-light conditions.

### After the inspection

1. **Upload to the cloud.** Connect the camera to WiFi. Photos transfer automatically to the album.
2. **Enhance dark images.** Use the AI Enhancement tool in the web app viewer to improve visibility in poorly lit captures (basements, utility rooms, enclosed spaces).
3. **Apply face blur** if the inspection took place in an occupied building, public area, or any environment where individuals may be identifiable. See Section 7 below.

## 5. Document Findings

Once your panoramas are uploaded and enhanced, use the web app to build your inspection record.

### Annotations

> **Feature highlight: Annotations [PREMIUM]** -- Place findings directly on panoramas with title, description, and precise position. Creates a visual map of all observations within each panorama. The primary tool for documenting defects, non-conformances, and items requiring follow-up.

Open a panorama in the 360 viewer and click on the point of interest to place an **annotation**. Each annotation includes:

- **Title:** A short label for the finding (e.g., "Crack in load-bearing wall", "Water damage, ceiling tile", "Fire extinguisher missing").
- **Description:** Details, measurements, severity level, or reference to the applicable regulation.

Place one annotation per finding. The annotation pins remain visible in the viewer, creating a visual map of all observations within each panorama.

You can also use the **drawing tool** to circle or highlight specific areas directly on the panorama for added clarity.

For annotation details, see: [Annotations and Markup](/en/daily-use/annotations-and-markup)

### AI Enhancement for low-light areas

> **Feature highlight: AI Enhancement [FREE]** -- Improves brightness and resolution on dark panoramas, making details visible that would otherwise be lost in shadows. Essential for basements, mechanical rooms, elevator shafts, and any space with poor lighting. No extra cost.

Inspections often include basements, mechanical rooms, elevator shafts, and other spaces with poor lighting. The AI Enhancement feature adjusts brightness and applies super resolution to make details visible that would otherwise be lost in shadows.

Apply enhancement from the viewer by selecting the Enhancement option. The processing runs in the cloud and typically completes within seconds.

For AI processing details, see: [AI Processing](/en/daily-use/ai-processing)

### Important note on annotations and sharing

Annotations are visible to all team members within the RICOH360 platform. However, when you share a panorama externally via a public link, annotations are not included in the shared view. Plan your sharing workflow accordingly (see Section 6).

## 6. Build the Evidence Package

### Before/after comparison

For recurring inspections or post-repair verification, use the **Side-by-Side comparison** in the viewer. Open a current panorama, activate Side-by-Side mode, and select the previous capture in the second panel. Navigate both views independently to compare conditions at the same location across time.

This is particularly valuable for:
- Pre-repair vs. post-repair documentation
- Year-over-year condition tracking
- Verifying that corrective actions were completed

For the comparison workflow, see: [Side-by-Side Comparison](/en/daily-use/side-by-side-comparison)

### Share inspection evidence

Generate a **share link** for individual panoramas to include in your reports, emails to clients, or insurance claim files. The recipient can view the full 360 panorama in their browser without a RICOH360 account.

For comprehensive access (multiple panoramas, annotations, full album), invite the reviewer as a **team member** on the platform.

### Export considerations

RICOH360 does not currently offer a native PDF export or a single link to share an entire album. For formal reports:

- **Link-based approach:** Paste individual panorama share links into your report document. The reader clicks through to the immersive 360 view.
- **Screenshot approach:** Use the Perspective Crop feature or take screenshots from specific angles within the viewer to include static images in your PDF report, supplemented by the 360 link for full context.

## 7. Privacy and Compliance

### Automatic face blurring

> **Feature highlight: Face Blur [FREE]** -- AI-powered detection and blurring of people in panoramas. Covers full persons (not just faces), so individuals are obscured even when facing away or partially visible. Essential for GDPR compliance in occupied buildings, public spaces, and healthcare facilities. No extra cost.

RICOH360 includes an AI-powered **Privacy Blur** feature that detects and blurs people in panoramic images. The detection covers full persons (not just faces), which means individuals are obscured even when facing away from the camera or partially visible.

### When to apply blur

Apply face blur whenever your panoramas may contain identifiable individuals:

- Inspections in **occupied buildings** (offices, residential units, healthcare facilities)
- Captures in **public spaces** (lobbies, corridors, retail areas)
- Any context where **GDPR or local privacy regulations** require anonymization of personal data in visual records

You can apply blur from the 360 viewer in the web app. The processing runs in the cloud and overwrites the original with the blurred version.

### Limitation

Privacy Blur applies to **panoramic images only**, not to 360 video. If your inspection workflow includes video capture, be aware that video content cannot be blurred through the platform.

## 8. Collaborate on Findings

### Internal collaboration

Inspection work often involves multiple people: the field inspector, the report writer, the quality manager, the project owner.

- **Team invitation [PREMIUM]:** Invite up to 10 colleagues per Business Pack license. All team members get full access to albums, annotations, projects, and the viewer.
- **Shared albums:** The inspector captures, the quality manager reviews the panoramas and annotations from the office. No file transfers needed.
- **Annotations for collaborative review [PREMIUM]:** Multiple team members can review the same panoramas and see each other's annotations. The quality manager can add comments to the inspector's findings, or flag items that need re-inspection.
- **Projects for recurring sites [PREMIUM]:** For facilities inspected annually or quarterly, a project with floor plans and markers lets any team member navigate directly to the relevant location, regardless of who performed the original capture.

### External collaboration

Share inspection evidence with clients, insurers, auditors, and regulatory bodies:

- **Public link sharing:** Generate a shareable link for an individual panorama. The recipient views the full 360-degree image in their browser, no account needed.
- **What external viewers see:** The complete panorama with pan and zoom navigation.
- **What external viewers do not see:** Annotations are stripped from shared links. Project views (floor plans with markers) are not accessible. Sharing is per individual panorama, not per album.

For formal evidence packages, combine share links with screenshots of annotated views in your report document.

### Using 360 in meetings

During findings review meetings or client debriefs, open the RICOH360 web app on a shared screen. Navigate the inspected space in real time, zoom into specific areas, and walk participants through findings in spatial context. This is significantly more effective than presenting a slideshow of flat photos and lets stakeholders ask "what about the area behind that wall?" with an immediate answer.

## 9. Tips from the Field

**Choose the right camera for the environment.** The THETA A1 (IP64) handles dusty construction sites, damp basements, and outdoor conditions. If your inspections regularly take you into harsh environments, this is the camera to deploy.

**HDR is essential in low light.** Basements, utility rooms, and enclosed mechanical spaces benefit enormously from HDR capture. The difference between a standard shot and an HDR shot in a dimly lit room is often the difference between usable evidence and an unusable dark image.

**Use a monopod for stability and consistency.** A monopod keeps the camera at a consistent height, reduces motion blur, and makes it easy to capture from the same position during repeat inspections. Mark capture positions on a site sketch for year-over-year consistency.

**Capture first, annotate later.** On-site time is valuable. Focus on systematic coverage during the inspection. Add annotations, enhancements, and face blur from the web app afterward, when you have time to be thorough.

**Name albums immediately.** With high-frequency inspection work (multiple sites per day), consistent album naming prevents confusion. Create and name the album before arriving on site so you can start capturing immediately.

**Keep a capture checklist.** For recurring inspection types, maintain a simple checklist of spaces or assets to capture. This prevents gaps in coverage and ensures consistent documentation across inspectors on the same team.
